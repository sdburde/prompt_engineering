10 — SYSTEM 2 ATTENTION PROMPTING
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

─────────────────────────────────────────
KEYWORDS
─────────────────────────────────────────

S2A            → System 2 Attention. Weston & Suber 2023.
                 Two steps: (1) rewrite question to remove bias/noise, (2) answer rewritten question.

Bias           → Irrelevant anecdotes, emotional framing, loaded assumptions in the question.
                 Example: "My grandfather, a genius, believed X. Don't you agree?"

Distractor     → Information in the problem that is irrelevant to solving it.
                 Example: "John is tall and works as a chef. He has 12 cookies..."
                 "tall" and "chef" are distractors for the math problem.

─────────────────────────────────────────
PATTERN
─────────────────────────────────────────

Step 1 — Neutralize:
  "Rewrite this question removing irrelevant context and emotional framing."

Step 2 — Answer:
  Answer the rewritten question only.

─────────────────────────────────────────
USE CASES
─────────────────────────────────────────

Opinion questions with loaded framing.
Math problems with distractors.
Customer questions with irrelevant backstory.
Any question where bias could skew the answer.

─────────────────────────────────────────
KEY INSIGHT
─────────────────────────────────────────

LLMs are sycophantic — they tend to agree with framing in the question.
S2A breaks the sycophancy by separating the question from its framing.
